Indian Army

The Indian Army is the world’s largest all-volunteer standing army, with approximately 1.45 million active personnel as of 2026. Every soldier serves by choice, and as of early 2026, the Global Firepower Index ranks the Indian military as the 4th most powerful in the world, following the USA, Russia, and China.

The army is a global leader in high-altitude warfare, maintaining control over the Siachen Glacier, the world’s highest battlefield. Its engineering excellence is demonstrated by the Bailey Bridge in Ladakh, which, at 18,380 feet, is the highest bridge in the world, built by the army in 1982.

Rooted in a legacy of bravery, the army’s highest wartime award is the Param Vir Chakra, which has been awarded only 21 times for exceptional valour. The army’s official motto, "Service Before Self" (Seva Paramo Dharma), guides every soldier's commitment to prioritizing national duty over personal safety.

The year 2026 has been designated by the Indian Army as the "Year of Networking and Data Centricity," focusing on digital transformation and AI-enabled warfare. This modernization effort includes the public debut of specialized units like the Bhairav Battalion, which focuses on drone-enabled and multi-domain operations.

Beyond combat, the army is a major contributor to United Nations peacekeeping missions, with thousands of troops deployed globally. It also leads massive humanitarian efforts, such as "Operation Rahat," which remains one of the largest civilian rescue operations ever conducted during natural disasters.

The Indian Army maintains a unique blend of tradition and modernity, exemplified by the 61st Cavalry, which is the world’s only functional non-ceremonial horse-mounted cavalry regiment still in service today. It also operates a highly specialized Army Dog Squad, where dogs are trained for tracking, explosives detection, and search and rescue in the harshest terrains. These canine soldiers have their own gallantry awards, highlighting the army's respect for all its members. Furthermore, the army is known for its eco-friendly initiatives, with "Eco-Battalions" dedicated solely to reforestation and environmental conservation across India’s border regions.
